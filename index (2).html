British Rule in India

From Colonization to Independence (1757 - 1947)

Introduction Timeline Major Effects Freedom Struggle

Introduction

The British Rule in India started in 1757 after the Battle of Plassey. Initially, the British East India Company controlled many regions. After the Revolt of 1857, the British Crown took direct control, marking the beginning of the "British Raj" that continued till India's independence in 1947.

Timeline

  • 1757: Battle of Plassey – Start of British dominance
  • 1857: Revolt of 1857 – First major resistance
  • 1858: British Crown takes control – Beginning of British Raj
  • 1885: Formation of Indian National Congress
  • 1919: Jallianwala Bagh Massacre
  • 1942: Quit India Movement
  • 1947: India gains independence

Major Effects of British Rule

  • Destruction of Indian traditional industries
  • Introduction of modern education and legal systems
  • Construction of railways, postal and telegraph systems
  • Social reforms like banning of Sati
  • Political unification of India under one administration

Freedom Struggle

The Indian freedom struggle was marked by both non-violent and revolutionary movements. Leaders like Mahatma Gandhi, Jawaharlal Nehru, Subhas Chandra Bose, and many others played vital roles. Key movements included the Non-Cooperation Movement, Civil Disobedience, and the Quit India Movement, all of which led to India achieving independence on August 15, 1947.
